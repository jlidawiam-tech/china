Yasmine Senhaji | Traditional Commerce

II. Traditional Commerce in China

1. Traditional Commerce

  • Local Markets & Small Shops: Neighborhood-based trading hubs where daily commerce occurred
  • Direct Seller-Client Relationship: Face-to-face interactions built on personal connections
  • Trust & Negotiation: High importance of bargaining skills and personal reputation (Guanxi)

2. Payment Methods

  • Primary Use of Cash: Physical currency was the standard for all transactions
  • Lack of Digitalization: No digital infrastructure or online payment systems existed
  • Slow & Insecure: Transactions were time-consuming and carrying cash posed security risks

3. Limitations

  • Limited Access: Consumers could only buy what was physically available nearby
  • Little Choice: Narrow variety of products compared to modern standards
  • Geographic Dependency: High reliance on physical location and travel to markets

Evolution Timeline: From Cash to Cashless

Pre-2000

Cash Dominance: 100% cash transactions. Liangpiao (粮票) rationing tickets for rice and oil.

2000-2010

Early Digital: Introduction of debit cards. Cash still dominant at 80% of transactions.

2011-2015

Mobile Leap: Alipay (2004) and WeChat Pay (2013) launch. Cash drops to 50%.

2016-2020

QR Revolution: Mobile payment exceeds cash. 86% smartphone payment penetration in cities.

2020-Present

Cashless Society: Cash represents less than 10% of transactions in tier-1 cities.

Source: People's Bank of China Payment System Development Report 2023

The Giants & The Digital Leap

The Mobile-First Leap

Unlike the West, China skipped the desktop computer era. The population moved directly from classic phones to smartphones, creating a mobile-first society where the smartphone is an extension of the self.

QR Code Revolution

The QR code payment is the foundation of the revolution. Cash has practically disappeared from big cities. This "one-click" ease removed all psychological barriers to purchase.

Super-App Ecosystem

WeChat (Tencent) and Alipay (Alibaba) are the OS of daily life. One app for chat, taxi, bills, and shopping. You never leave the ecosystem.

Alibaba Group

The pioneer with Taobao (C2C) and Tmall (B2C). Focuses on building the "operating system" of commerce via cloud and logistics.

JD.com

The "Amazon of China." Controls its own massive logistics network, prioritizing quality, speed, and authentic luxury goods.

Pinduoduo (PDD)

The disruptor. Introduced "Team Buying" and gamification, successfully capturing lower-tier city demographics and rural markets.

Hema (Freshippo) | New Retail

Jack Ma's flagship concept that erases the line between physical and digital: scan products with your phone to see farm-to-table origin, visible logistics rails on the ceiling where order bags circulate, and 30-minute delivery within 3km. The store is the fulfillment center.

Seamless Fulfillment

Cainiao Network

Not a classic transport company, but a data platform connecting all logistics actors to optimize routes. It orchestrates, others execute.

100+ billion parcels annually (China State Post Bureau 2024)

Smart Warehousing

AGV robots (Automated Guided Vehicles) sort 24/7 with near-zero error. Big Data predicts demand: products are routed to nearby warehouses before you order.

Live Shopping Integration

When an influencer sells 10,000 lipsticks in 2 minutes, warehouses are already packaging during the live stream. Extreme lean flow.

JD.com Promise

Delivery before 11am for any order placed before midnight. Vertical integration: own trucks, warehouses, and delivery fleet.

Last Mile Innovations

Fengchao smart lockers in residences, drone delivery to rural areas, L4 autonomous vehicles on campuses, biodegradable packaging.

Social Commerce Revolution

What is Social Commerce?

A form of online commerce that uses social networks to sell directly to consumers. Unlike classic e-commerce, you can buy without leaving apps like Instagram or TikTok.

Livestreaming Commerce

A seller or influencer presents products in live video, explains advantages, answers questions. Viewers can buy instantly. This model works exceptionally well in China because consumers love interaction and trust influencers.

Singles' Day Record

€100M+

Some top streamers sell this amount in a single live session during the "11.11" festival on platforms like Douyin (Chinese TikTok).

Advantages

  • Interactive experience
  • Fast purchase process
  • Builds consumer trust

Risks

  • Impulse purchases
  • Risk of counterfeit products
  • Limited-time discounts create pressure

Pinduoduo Model

Team Buying: Prices drop when you share offers with friends/family. This social revolution successfully captured rural markets and less affluent populations, turning shopping into a viral game.

Impacts, Data & Employment

Personal Data

Platforms collect massive information: habits, preferences, location to propose adapted products.

Advantages: Personalized recommendations, time savings

Risks: Loss of privacy, surveillance, abusive data use

"The Convenience Paradox"

Employment Transformation

New jobs created: Delivery riders, influencers, customer service employees

But also: Precarious conditions, difficult working conditions (riders facing algorithmic pressure), disappearance of traditional retail jobs

Digital Dependency

  • Impulse buying behavior
  • Strong algorithmic influence
  • High screen time and online addiction

Wiam Arbouch | Strategic Rationale

Why China?

After presenting the various evolutions of digital commerce in China, it is important to explain the reasons for our choice of this topic.

We chose China because it is today one of the world leaders in e-commerce.

This country has undergone a rapid transformation, moving from traditional commerce to a very advanced digital system based on mobile technologies, social networks, and fast delivery.

China is also a unique example of constant innovation, with solutions like social commerce, live streaming, and super-apps that profoundly change consumer habits.

Studying this country allows us to understand how digital transforms the economy, society, and lifestyles, while giving a glimpse of the future of global commerce.

Key Takeaways

  • Uncontested global leader
  • Unique rapid transformation
  • Constant innovation model
  • Blueprint for future commerce

Wiam Jlida | Future Horizon

Neural Commerce 2075

Zero-Tap Consumption

Beyond the "Click": AI-driven fulfillment based on neural and biometric data.

Anticipatory Needs: Products arrive before the consumer even realizes the need.

Passive Utility: Shopping becomes a background service, like water or electricity.

The Death of Distance

Molecular Manufacturing: 3D "in-home" printing of goods via digital licenses.

Underground Logistics: Hyper-loop vacuum tubes replacing traditional delivery trucks.

Instant Gratification: The gap between "desire" and "possession" drops to zero.

The "Phygital" Metaverse

Haptic Shopping: VR boutiques where users can "feel" textures and weight.

Digital Twins: Ultra-precise avatars for perfect virtual fittings and trials.

Social Credit 2.0: Consumption habits integrated into real-time social/carbon scoring.

The Grand Paradox: Efficiency vs Agency

The Loss of Discovery: Does total prediction kill the joy of the "find"?

The Privacy Trade-off: Convenience in exchange for 24/7 biometric surveillance.

Free Will: In a world of perfect algorithms, do we still choose our own desires?

Conclusion

"Commerce is no longer an action we take; it is a current that flows around us. China has shown that when you remove all friction, digital trade becomes as essential as air."

The Borderless Ecosystem

China's e-commerce model is the global blueprint for "New Retail." By merging social life, entertainment, and logistics into a single digital layer, they have created a high-frequency ecosystem that rewards innovation but demands agility.

The next frontier lies in "Cross-border" expansion (Temu, Shein) and AI-driven hyper-personalization.
